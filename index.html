A little about me: I work as a research assistant under the supervision of Prof. Chandra R. Murthy in the Electrical Communication Engineering department at the Indian Institute of Science. I graduated from BITS Pilani, India in 2020 with a BE degree in electrical and electronics engineering. My bachelor's thesis was supervised by Prof. Chandra.

Research summary: My research centers around the impact of user mobility on the performance of modern wireless technologies. Temporal variations in the channel response that arise due to moving users cause a disparity between the estimated channel and the true channel. This is known as channel aging. I aim to characterise the consequent drop in the achievable performance measure (per-user rate or sum rate) as a function of mobility. Two major settings that I have considered so far are:

  • Cell-free massive MIMO: A distributed variant of cellular massive MIMO, cell-free massive MIMO offers higher coverage probability to users at the cost of increased backhaul. Currently, I am investigating the impact of channel aging and pilot contamination on the downlink performance of CF-mMIMO for two cases: (i) users learn the downlink channel using pilots sent by the APs.
    (ii) users rely on long term channel statistics to decode the data symbols. This is a joint work with Prof. Ribhu Chopra at IIT Guwahati and Prof. Chandra at IISc.
  • OFDMA-based massive MIMO: In multicarrier systems that rely on orthogonality, mobility brings another major impairment - inter subcarrier interference (ICI). In my bachelor's thesis, I looked at the joint effect of subcarrier allocation and user mobility on the uplink performance of massive MIMO-OFDMA systems for two receive combining schemes: maximal ratio (MR) and zero-forcing (ZF). We got some nice results, and wrote submitted a journal paper.
My work entails using tools from statistical signal processing, wireless communications and (a little bit of) information theory.
